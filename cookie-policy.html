Cookie Policy

Home Cookie Policy

Last updated: June 2026

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. Similar technologies, such as local storage and analytics tags, can also store or access information on your device. These technologies help websites work properly, remember certain choices, and understand how visitors use the site.

2. How ClientFlow uses cookies and similar technologies

ClientFlow uses essential storage to keep the website working and to remember your cookie choice. ClientFlow also uses optional analytics technology, including Google Analytics, to understand website traffic, page performance, and successful contact form submissions.

Optional analytics technology is only activated after you choose “Accept All” in the cookie popup. If you choose “Reject Optional”, Google Analytics is not loaded by ClientFlow.

3. Essential cookies and local storage

Essential cookies and local storage are needed for the website to function properly. This includes remembering your cookie preference so the popup does not keep appearing on every page visit.

ClientFlow stores your cookie choice in your browser using local storage under the name clientflowCookieChoice. This stores whether you selected “accepted” or “rejected”. It does not store your name, email address, phone number, or message content.

4. Optional analytics cookies

If you select “Accept All”, ClientFlow loads Google Analytics. Google Analytics helps ClientFlow understand how visitors use the website, including page views, traffic sources, general engagement, and successful contact form submissions.

For example, ClientFlow may measure when a visitor submits the contact form successfully. This event is used to understand whether the website is generating enquiries. ClientFlow does not send the visitor’s name, email address, phone number, business name, or message content to Google Analytics.

5. Google Analytics

ClientFlow uses Google Analytics 4 with the measurement ID G-5SS92R1R1T. Google Analytics may set or use analytics cookies and similar technologies after you accept optional cookies.

The information collected through Google Analytics is used to understand website performance, improve the website experience, and assess which pages or channels help generate enquiries. This may include information such as pages visited, approximate location, device type, browser, traffic source, and interaction events.

6. Contact form tracking

When you submit the contact form successfully, ClientFlow may record an analytics event called lead_submit. This helps ClientFlow understand that the website generated an enquiry.

The analytics event does not include the personal information entered into the form. The form content itself is sent through Web3Forms so ClientFlow can receive and respond to your enquiry by email.

7. Managing your cookie choice

You can accept or reject optional cookies using the cookie popup. After making a choice, the cookie icon remains available in the bottom-left corner of the website so you can reopen the settings.

If you want to change your choice completely, you can also clear this website’s local storage and cookies in your browser settings, then reload the website and choose again.

8. Browser controls

You can control or delete cookies through your browser settings. Blocking or deleting cookies may affect how certain parts of the website work, including whether your cookie preference is remembered.

9. Updates to this policy

ClientFlow may update this Cookie Policy from time to time, especially if new analytics, advertising, automation, or website features are added.

10. Contact

If you have any questions about this Cookie Policy, you can contact ClientFlow at: hello@clientflow.org.uk
